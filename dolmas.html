Dolmas, Persian Style

                    

Ingredients

  • Grape Leaves                 ~50 pieces
  • Rice                                    2 cups
  • Barberries                        1/2 cup
  • Dried Split Chickpeas      1/2 cup
  • Ground Beef                       1 lb
  • Diced Onion                        1 large
  • Herbs*                                2 cups
  • Turmeric                              2 Tbsp
  • Sugar                               1/4 cups
  • Vinegar                             3/4 cups
  • Dried Mint                         1/4 cup
  • Yoghurt                             1/2 cup
  • Vegetable oil                     1/4 cup
  • Butter                 1/4 cup
  • Lemon Juice                                1/8 cup
  • Salt and Pepper

*Herbs

Having aromatic fresh herbs is critical, however the exact mix is not that important. You can be creative and choose any mix of the below combinations. You can also choose ideally fresh herbs and spend 5 hours cleaning and dicing them, or you can lower your quality expectations and use dried herbs, or if you want to achieve both ease and quality, you can use fresh herb mixed (frozen mostly which is ok) available in Persian / Middle Eastern Grocery Stores. Example Product Picture below:

If you don't find the exact dolma mix you are looking for, you can use any other mix available.

Equipments

  • Deep nonstick pan 1 or 2 depending on the size.
  • Mixing bowl.

Process

Prepare the Mix!

  • Half-cook the rice in boling water ~ 5 mins.

  • Half-cook the dried split peas in boiling water ~ 10 mins.

  • Saute onions in oil until brown ~ 5 mins, keep mixing otherwise will burn.

  • Add turmeric to the onion and saute for 2 more mins.
  • Add ground beef to the onion and sauter for ~ 5 mins.
  • Add salt and pepper.
  • Add the half-cooked dried split peas and sauter for 2-3 mins.
  • Add lemon juice and adjust flavor by adding salt and pepper as needed.
  • Add the half-cooked rice
  • Add the mixed herbs, mix and the take off the stove.
  • Let the mix cool for ~10 mins.
  • Fold in the yoghurt gradually trying not to make the rice get too mushy. The yoghurt is critical to keep the mixture intact.
  • Our mix is ready. Time to wrap the dolmas, but before that, let's set up our pot.
  • Add some oil to the bottom of your depp non-stick pan.
  • Put one layer of leaves on the bottom of your pan covering it fully. Some overlap is fine. Use the cracked or torn leaves, leave the intact ones for the actual dolmas.

Wrapping dolmas!

  • Pick one leave and place it flat in your left-hand palm with root on top as below:

                    

  • Put one large spoon of material in the middle as picture above.
  • Wrap the dolma by following a routine of starting from top and continuing with the middle and finishing up with the below part of the leave. Keep it tight and make sure there are no openings.
  • The seal would be facing up in this situation, pace the dolma on the pan bottom while flipping it, putting the seal down.
  • Follow the last 4 steps and place them tightly next to each other.
  • Follow with the rest of dolmas.
  • It's ok to have 2 layers. More than 2 is not suggested. If you have more, use 2 pots!
  • When all dolmas are placed, add yet another layer of leaves on top making it flat.
  • Place a ceramic plate on top and add a bowl filled with water on top. Do not fill the bowl as we don't want it to spill into the mix. You could just use any other heaving object you feel comfortable with rather than the bowl. For example a cast iron burger press if it fits. The idea is to press the whole thing down while it cooks.

Cooking Dolmas - 1st round

  • Finally time to cook!
  • Bring temperatue to Medium.
  • After a minute or two when you hear some cooking action happing, add 1/4 cup of water to the pot and close the lid immediately. This makes steam create and stay in place.
  • After a minute or two when you ensured the steam engine is doing its magic, lower the temperature to low.
  • Let it cook for ~ 30 mins while we make the sauce!

Sauce

  • Add dried mint and ground saffron to 1 stick of butter and melt by placing into microwave.
  • When the butter is melted, add the vinegar and adjust the flavor by adding salt and pepper as needed.

Cooking Dolmas - 2nd round

  • Now remove the water cup (or weight) from the top of dolmas and be careful not to burn yourself!
  • You can remove the top layer of leaves now or just leave them there, if you leave them, try to make some holes in them letting the sauce to find its place.
  • Add the sauce by spoon trying to cover all the parts of the dolmas, leave some sauce for the serving.
  • Cook for an extra 30 mins to 1 hour.
  • Take dolmas carefully out try not to break them and put on the serving dish.
